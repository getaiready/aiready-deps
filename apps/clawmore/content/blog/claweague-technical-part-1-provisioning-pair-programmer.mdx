# Technical: Provisioning Your Pair Programmer in 60 Seconds

> The first step in adding a **Claweague** to your team is giving them a place to work. In ClawMore, that means a dedicated, isolated AWS account and a private Hub-and-Spoke repository.

---

## The Infrastructure Handshake

Traditional AI tools share a single multitenant infrastructure. This is a security nightmare and an performance bottleneck. A true **Claweague** needs its own "Office"—an isolated sandbox where it can execute code, provision its own serverless functions, and manage its own secrets.

### How it Works: One-Click AWS Vending

When you onboard a new Claweague, our `provision-node.ts` orchestrator triggers a massive, automated workflow:

1. **AWS Account Vending**: We use AWS Organizations to find or create a fresh, isolated account from a "warm pool."
2. **Governance Enforcement**: We apply **Serverless-only SCPs** (Service Control Policies) to ensure your agent never accidentally spends $10,000 on a massive RDS instance.
3. **Repo Provisioning**: We create a private repository from our `serverlessclaw` template in the `clawmost` organization.

## The "Secret Injection" Phase

Once the account and repo are ready, we inject the necessary credentials into your GitHub Actions secrets via the SST orchestration layer. This includes:

- `SPOKE_OPENAI_API_KEY` (or Anthropic/Minimax)
- `SPOKE_GITHUB_TOKEN`
- `AWS_CREDENTIALS` (scoped to the new account)

## Verifying the Setup

Within 60 seconds, your new teammate is live. You can verify the health of your new "Spoke" directly from the ClawMore dashboard.

```bash
# Verify your new agent's connection
npx @aiready/cli status --spoke my-claweague-1
```
